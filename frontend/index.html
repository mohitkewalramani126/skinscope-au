Before you begin

SkinScope AU is a public awareness demo, not a medical device. It does not diagnose skin cancer, and a low-risk result does not rule it out. It can be wrong in both directions, and it is measurably less sensitive on darker skin tones.

What it is

Two ML models: one outlines the spot (segmentation), one estimates a calibrated risk score, plus a Q&A assistant that answers only from cited sources, behind a safety gate that refuses diagnostic questions.

What it is not

A diagnosis. At its low-false-alarm operating point the risk model misses roughly half of malignant cases. A fairness audit also found lower sensitivity on darker skin tones, so treat those results with extra caution.

What to actually do

Build a monthly skin-check habit: same light, same angle, photos for comparison. Then see a GP or dermatologist about anything new, changing, or concerning. That judgement belongs to them, not to this tool.
